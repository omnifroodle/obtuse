We should not separate governmental powers, such as the legislative and executive powers.
No single form of government is appropriate for all circumstances.
Representative democracy is preferable to direct democracy.
The general will could be assessed by referendum if (1) every voter was fully informed on the issues under vote, and (2) voters did not communicate with each other on the issues. Governance by the general will ensures that citizens are at least as free, under a social contract, as they are in a state of nature.
